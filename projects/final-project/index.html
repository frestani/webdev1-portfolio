ADHD

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder

What is ADHD? It is a neurodevelopmental disorder that typically begins in childhood and may persist into adulthood. The disorder makes it difficult for individuals to pay attention, complete tasks in a timely manner, and includes hyperactivity and impulsivity.

Symptoms

  • Inattention: Individuals struggle with staying focused, have difficulty completing tasks, and find it hard to be organized.
  • Hyperactivity: People with ADHD may move around constantly, fidget, and have excessive energy.
  • Impulsivity: Those who have ADHD are more likely to struggle with self-control, controlling their actions, or thinking about long-term and short-term effects of their actions.

Types of ADHD

  • Inattentive Type: Individuals struggle more with paying attention, reigning in their thoughts, and get distracted frequently.
  • Hyperactive-Impulsive Type: Those with this type of ADHD have more symptoms of hyperactivity and impulsivity, such as engaging in risk-taking behaviors, difficutly sitting still, and controlling outbursts.
  • Combination Type: People who have combination-type ADHD share symptoms of the inattentive type and the hyperactive type.

Treatment

  • Therapy: Therapy is the first option when it comes to treating/managing ADHD. People learn how to cope with executive dysfunction, impulse control, regulating behaviors/moods, and more.
  • Medication: Medication is another option for treating ADHD. There are a wide vareity of medications for ADHD, with stimulants being one of the more popular forms of medication.
